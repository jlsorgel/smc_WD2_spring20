Endangered Species International is strongly committed to reversing the trend of human-induced species extinction, saving endangered animals, and preserving wild places.

Plants and animals are part of our unique heritage, they keep us alive, purifying water, fixing nitrogen, recycling nutrients and waste, and pollinating crops. They are vital for the physical and spiritual well-being offering us a colorful and rich world.

More than 31,000 species are threatened with extinction. That is 27% of all assessed species.

We focus on species that have the biggest chance to become extinct including those that do not attract the media attention. Their ecosystems as a whole are a crucial component of our conservation strategy. We also work hard on saving endangered species found in various types of habitat and serving as protecting umbrella to other endangered species.

African Wild Dog

One of the world’s most endangered mammals.They are particularly susceptible to being caught as bycatch in the snares set by poachers. If one dog gets caught in a snare, the rest of the pack are most likely to go back to find the missing individual. An entire pack can be killed.

Population Remaing: 1,400

Vaquita

Vaquita, the world's rarest marine mammal, is on the edge of extinction.. Vaquita are often caught and drowned in gillnets used by illegal fishing operations in marine protected areas within Mexico's Gulf of California. The population has dropped drastically in the last few years.

Population Remaing: ~10

Polar Bears

Because of ongoing and potential loss of their sea ice habitat resulting from climate change, polar bears were listed as a threatened species in the US under the Endangered Species Act in May 2008.

Pygmy Three-toed Sloths

While their native island is uninhabited, seasonal visitors, including fishermen, lobster divers, and indigenous people, have been known to hunt the sloths. Ongoing destruction of its habitat and the sloths’ recent fame has also made them a target for capture and export for public display.

Population Remaing:~ 48

Sumatran Elephant

In 2012, the Sumatran elephant was changed from “Endangered” to “Critically Endangered” because half of its population has been lost in one generation—a decline that is largely due to habitat loss and as a result human-elephant conflict.

Population Remaing: 2,400-2,800

Western Gorilla

Because of poaching and disease, the gorilla’s numbers have declined by more than 60% over the last 20 to 25 years. Even if all of the threats to western lowland gorillas were removed, scientists calculate that the population would require some 75 years to recover.

Population Remaing: Unknown
